FAQ
Why should I participate in the Blueprint Case Competition?
Lots of reasons!

1) To hone your skills as a critical thinker. Whether you’re interested in business, medicine, tech, or any other field, the Blueprint Case Competition will push you out of your comfort zone to deconstruct and think through challenging problems.

2) To work closely with leading experts in the healthcare industry. Each team will be individually paired with an adult mentor who will coach you through the process of thinking through the case study.

3) To tackle real-world problems. Unlike other consulting marathons, all of our cases are directly sourced from real nonprofits in the healthcare industry. The solutions you come up with may actually inform some tangible change!

4) To be on the cusp of an exciting new initiative. BCC will be Stanford’s first undergraduate case competition and one of few undergrad events dedicated to the unique intersection of healthcare and business. We hope to introduce more students to the exciting world of case consulting!

5) To win some awesome prizes and have lots of fun!

What is a case competition?
A case competition is an event during which participants work in teams to develop the best approach to solving a provided case study (case study = any problem explained in a detailed report). Think of it kind of like a hackathon, but where all the solutions are proposals/action items/strategic recommendations rather than technical software projects.
What kind of preparation do I need to do before the day of?
Not much! We’ll send out an email on Wednesday, March 29 with final team groupings so you can briefly get in touch with any of your teammates you don’t know. That email will also contain a PDF of the case study you’ll be working on. We ask that you make sure to read the document over prior to the competition, but all case work will happen in-person on April 9.
What should I bring?
Bring your laptop, charger, and something to write with. Dress is business casual (think slacks/skirt and a button down).
What if this will be my first case competition?
Not to worry. We’ve specifically designed this competition with people like you in mind. In addition to providing access to a personal mentor, we’ve scheduled time on the day of the competition for a series of educational sessions—including a workshop by Stanford Consulting —designed for students at varying skill levels.
